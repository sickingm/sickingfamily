Ode to a Mark

Hark!
What light through yonder window breaks?
'Tis the eighth,
And like to the Lark
At break of day arising
(Bird thou never wert)
On such a day we will our celebration keep according to thy birth.
Which bounteous gift wouldst thou in bounty cherish?
The ravin'd salt-sea
Shark?
The star to every wand'ring
Bark?
Perchance, the root of hemlock digg'd i' the
Dark?
On what stuff of mine hast thou
Embark'd?
A poem unlimited,
That is all, but I would fain write it once
You ask for whom we stand this special party?
For whimpled, whining, purblind, wayward boy?
For senior-junior, giant-dwarf?
O no! it is our ever fixéd
Mark.

-- by Regie Neff, Will Shakespeare, and a little PB Shelley 3/8/08
