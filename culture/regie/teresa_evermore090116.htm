Teresa Evermore

Once, in middle January, while I pondered, very merry,
Over the lengthy list of family birthdays by the score,
Sibs arrived for celebration, cake with candles, great elation,
Riotous Sicking salutations, animation as of years before.
"Where is Teresa, though," we queried, "Why not here? Wherefore?"
Only us, and no one more.

All began to search together, heedless of the biting weather,
Looking for the dear but missing birthday honoree
Under chairs and under tables, under coats of mink, and sables,
In the attic, under gables, able not to find her. Where is she?!
Then a voice was heard so faintly, sweet above the riot, saintly,
Quoth the speaker, "I am she!"

Taunt us not, oh voice, away now! Let your false notes not betray now
Us into an error where her presence may not be!
So we search, on hoping, looking, though the food has finished cooking,
Though the beer has been partooken, tooken by the DBC.
Once again above the clamor, boldly now, without a stammer,
Cries the speaker, "I AM she!"

And Teresa, never flitting, still is sitting, still is sitting,
Waiting for some sense to come to loved ones such as us,
Though the cake is fast a-crumbling, still we hear the voice a-mumbling,
O'er the sound of family stumbling, rumbling as we make a fuss.
Saying yet, with resignation, through the end of celebration,
Quoth Teresa, "I am she."

-- Regie 1/16/09
