Snow, My Love

  
; I awake this morning to snowfall – the anticipated shock of last night’s forecast: well-known, but still surprise.

Reminding me of you.    

        
  It transforms the land from graying autumn to vibrant
magic – revealing dormant life now veiled. A paradox in white.

As I consider you.    

  
  Its unassuming beauty slakes my eyes. The landscape, now a dreamscape, with edges rounded, corners softened, hard lines smoothed.

As life can be with you.    

  
  Blanketing the peace with gentle susurration, its hushed and happy hoary hymn:
“All’s well, and well will be.”

As I ever hear from you.
