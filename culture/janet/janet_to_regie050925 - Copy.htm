Regie's Birthday 2005

I finally flew home from Dallas through Salt Lake
With a tired body and a queasy stomach-ache.

The mail was piled high, there were messages galore
the kitchen was straightened but with dirt on the floor.

I opened the mail, unpacked my bags
Threw clothes in the washer to clean my traveling rags.

Came upstairs with the thought of going to bed
But checked the computer for messages instead.

To my delight I found a poem so witty with rhyme
Summing up the birthday party where we all had a good time.

It's family and memories that mean the most
Even if it involves traveling from St. Louis and the west coast.

When you live far away it's nice to be included
Which is why Paul and I decided not to stay secluded.

It's a big deal to turn 50, it happens only once
As I shall see for myself in only a few months.

It seemed you were surprised, never expected a thing,
So having family show up was the best present we could bring.

Having siblings so funny sharing poems and stories
Overshadowed the Dodie's owner who was consumed with trivial worries.

It won't be too long Regie before we see you again
I hope it's in April when Libby marries a Nguyen. (nu-when)

-- Janet 9/25/05
