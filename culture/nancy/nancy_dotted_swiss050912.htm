An Ode to Dotted Swiss

For many years I was alone,
Surrounded by the boys.
I couldn't stand their stupid games,
And they hated all my toys.

I started praying for a sister
And it took me 8 long years,
But finally God sent Regie
To dry my constant tears.

What a darling little sister!
As cute as she could be.
We shared a room but never clothes,
She was too small for me.

Then while I wasn't looking
As often children do,
Regie started growing.
She grew and grew and grew.

And then one day while browsing
Through our closet for a skirt,
The color pink just caught my eye.
Regie's brand new shirt!

Dotted Swiss and pink it was
With roll-up sleeves to boot.
And dainty little buttons
Just made the whole thing cute.

I took it out and tried it on.
It was as cute as it could be.
Oh, thank you so much Regie,
For lending it to me.

Sometimes I would wear it
And sometimes Regie would.
But neither one could manage
To keep that blouse for good.

When one would want to wear it,
The other had it on.
And if I ever slept too late,
You could bet the blouse was gone.

One time I wrapped it up
And gave it as a gift
To Regie on her birthday
Just to see her spirits lift.

But now the pretty thing is gone.
It no longer fit the bill.
The collar drooped, the buttons lost.
We gave it to Good Will.

And now our Regie's 50.
And I am 58.
I thought another shirt to share
Would make her birthday great.

I went to Joann's Fabrics
In search of Dotted Swiss.
I looked and looked and asked the girl
But all I found was this.

A silly little present
With a theme for DBC
But if Mom should ever ask you,
It didn't come from me.

It's just a little something
To make your birthday super
I bet you can't believe I found
A Reindeer Pocket Pooper

But Reg-
If ever you feel gloomy
And want to conjure joy and bliss
Just remember all the happy days
We shared the Dotted Swiss.

-- Nancy 9/12/05
