Dear Mark and Matt:

For your birthday poem
I'm sure you still wait,
So I have to say sorry
That I'm a day late.

I'd have a nice home
On the island of Fiji
If I could write verse
Like our sister, Regie.

But I'm not a Shakespeare
Or even a Scott,
And I'm sorry to say
A poet, I'm not.

But you have to admit
That my words are spelled right,
And to catch my real meaning
You needn't be bright.

I want you to know
That I'm sending my wishes
For a sixtieth year
That's chock full of riches.

So, with a wave of the flag
and a tip of the hat,
"Happy Birthday," I say
To Mark and to Matt.

-- Nancy 3/8/08
